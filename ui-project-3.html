ux/ui casestudie: jar genius

Jar Genius is a user-centered mobile application concept designed to help users manage their pantry more effectively. Developed as part of an assignment to create a digital twin, the project focuses on mirroring the real-life state of a user's pantry in a digital format. By keeping track of pantry contents in an intuitive and accessible way, the application helps reduce food waste, avoid duplicate purchases, and simplify everyday grocery planning.

about the project

This project was conducted during the spring of 2024. The development of Jar Genius followed a structured design process rooted in user research and iterative prototyping. Starting with a broad exploration of the topic through literature review, mind mapping, brainstorming and sketching. The project gradually narrowed focus through interviews, persona development and low-fidelity prototypes.

Later stages included participatory workshops and feedback loops with peers and stakeholders, ensuring the solution was grounded in real-world needs. The final concept centers around empowering users with better visibility into their pantry, minimizing food waste, and simplifying shopping routines.

problem statement

In modern households, food waste is a persistent problem, often caused by poor visibility of existing pantry items. Users frequently buy duplicates or fail to notice products nearing expiration. Interviews revealed that pantry mismanagement, especially for dry goods and perishables, leads to significant waste. Users also struggle with assuming jars and containers are full when they are not. This highlighted the need for a simple, efficient, and engaging solution to support better pantry organization and smarter grocery habits.

final prototype

conclusion

The design process for Jar Genius demonstrated the importance of combining structured research with creative ideation and user feedback. Iterative development stages, including participatory workshops and prototype testing, ensured the final concept addressed genuine user needs. While the project identified areas for future improvement, such as even earlier market analysis and clearer documentation, the resulting application concept provides a strong foundation for reducing food waste and making pantry management easier and more intuitive for users.
